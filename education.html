Biography

Dr. Lei Chu received his M.S. and Ph.D. degrees from UESTC and SJTU, in 2015 and 2018, respectively. Now, he has been a research associate in the Department of Electrical Engineering at the University of Southern California since Sep. 2021. As an interdisciplinary researcher, my research interests include integrating information theory into neural network optimization and the related Wireless Communications and Smart Sensing applications.

1) Machine Learning(ML): First-order Optimization; Convergence Analysis; Domain Adaptation; Predictive Learning; Transformers; Information Theory guided Representation Learning; Physic-inspired Neural Network (NN); Random Matrix Theory; Theoretical NN Generalization Evaluation.

2) ML for Communications and Sensing: Semantic Localization; Directional Spectrum Sensing; 1-bit Massive MIMO; V2V Propagation Modeling; Channel Estimation/Prediction; V2V Resource Allocation; Joint Communication and Sensing.

3) ML for Sciences: Activity Recognition/Reconstruction with Wearables/Radar/WIFI; Intelligent Sensing with PMU, IMU, Electroencephalogram (EEG) and Point Clouds.

Academic Services

He authored over fifty papers, holds several patents, and contributed to three book chapters. He regularly reviews technical papers, completing 100+ peer reviews for 20+ SCI-indexed journals, including 10+ IEEE Transactions. He received the 2015 Distinguished Master's Thesis Award, the 2020 SJTU Doctoral Excellence Award, and a 2020 International Postdoctoral Fellowship. He serves a TPC member for several conferences and an academic/associate editor for three SCI-indexed journals. He presented a tutorial at IEEE GLOBECOM 2022 and emphasizes reproducible research by sharing his code with the public.
