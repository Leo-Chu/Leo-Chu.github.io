Overview

As an interdisciplinary researcher, my research interests include integrating information theory into neural network optimization and the related Wireless Communications and Smart Sensing applications.

1) Machine Learning(ML):

First-order Optimization; Convergence Analysis; Domain Adaptation; Predictive Learning; Transformers; Information Theory guided Representation Learning; Physic-inspired Neural Network (NN); Random Matrix Theory; Theoretical NN Generalization Evaluation.

Example Work I:

Exploiting information theory criterion for deep domain adaptation: unified framework and convergence analysis. Under review.

Example Work I:

Meta-PredRNN: Addressing the Course of Accumulative Prediction Error. Under review.

Example Work III:

Unlocking Semantic Localization: The Synergy of Classification and Regression in Deep Contextual Learning. Under review.

2) PSG-ML for Communications and Sensing:

Semantic Localization; Directional Spectrum Sensing; 1-bit Massive MIMO; V2V Propagation Modeling; Channel Estimation/Prediction; V2V Resource Allocation; Joint Communication and Sensing.

Example Work I:

Chu, Lei, Abdullah Alghafis, and Andreas F. Molisch. "Exploiting semantic localization in highly dynamic wireless networks using deep homoscedastic domain adaptation." arXiv preprint arXiv:2310.07792 (2023).

Example Work II:

Example Work III:

Chu, L., Wen, F., & Qiu, R. C. (2019). Eigen-inference precoding for coarsely quantized massive MU-MIMO system with imperfect CSI. IEEE Transactions on Vehicular Technology, 68(9), 8729-8743.

3) ML (with zero-/few- shot setting) for Sciences:

Activity Recognition/Reconstruction with Wearables/Radar/WIFI; Intelligent Sensing with PMU, IMU, Electroencephalogram (EEG) and Point Clouds.
